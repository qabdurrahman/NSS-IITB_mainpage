Who Are We?

The National Service Scheme at IIT Bombay is the largest student volunteer organization on campus, dedicated to social and community service. We offer students opportunities to make a positive impact on society through various departments, from education for the underprivileged to using technology to solve social issues. Our primary goal is spreading happiness through community service, creating joyful moments for campus residents through innovative activities. Being part of NSS brings happiness to everyone involved.

We aim to bring about a positive change in the society with focus on Education and Development of the underprivileged, conserving Nature and sowing the seeds of Sustainability. More than 350 volunteers guided by a team of 40 members carry out several initiatives throughout the year. We also work in close collaboration with several NGOs and Governmental bodies to reach out to more and more beneficiaries.

News Highlights

Initiatives

  • Flare

    FLARE is the Flagship event of NSS IIT Bombay. It is a series of online competitions, workshops and social events with participants from 30000+ schools and colleges Pan-India.

  • Mangrove Clean-Up

    Mangrove Clean-Up aims to preserve and protect the vital mangrove ecosystems. Volunteers participate in clean-up drives to maintain biodiversity and raise environmental conservation awareness. These efforts contribute significantly to the sustainability of our natural surroundings and promote ecological responsibility among the campuscommunity.

  • Field Visit

    We organized a skit and a demonstration to highlight how over irrigation and unregulated traditional methods of irrigation leads to low crop yield, pollution of groundwater resources, and how use of innovative technology can solve such issues.

  • Orphanage Visits

    Orphanage visits focus on providing care, support, and educational resources to children in need. Volunteers spend time with the children, helping with their studies, organizing fun activities, and offering mentorship to nurture their growth and development. These visits aim to create a supportive environment where children feel valued and motivated to pursuetheirdreams.

  • Plantation Drive

    The Plantation Drive focuses on increasing green cover and promoting environmental sustainability. Volunteers participate in tree-planting activities across campus and nearby areas, aiming to create a healthier and more eco-friendly environment. These drives also educate participants on the importance of biodiversity and environmental stewardship, fostering a sense of responsibilitytowardsnature.

  • Voice For Purpose

    The next educational portal of NSS IIT Bombay is here, this one catering to blind people. Voice For Purpose, wherein we upload audio books of various popular novels and stories. Visit the Channel to know more

  • Graduation Collection Drive

    "Happiness comes in byte-sized pieces, some small, some large. It infects one person, and spreads like a ripple, till it becomes an epidemic. We rise by lifting others." NSS IIT Bombay continues its struggle to give more meaning to someone's graduation and to someone's need.

  • UMMEED

    We teach the kids of construction workers from the labor camp and are grateful to our volunteers for sparking this 'UMMEED' in these young hearts.

  • Permaculture

    Whenever there's been fusion between science and nature, the product has always been amazing. Permaculture is one such thing where the agriculture is just made more fruitful not by chemicals but by utilising the elements of natur itself, be it the sun, or wind, or soil.

  • The Wild Side of IIT-Bombay

    We had a "TALK". The study, led by Dr Quadros alongwith WWF (World Wildlife Fund), continues to be relevant to this day as the sole source of information on the flora and fauna of the campus.

  • Old Age Home Visits

    Visits to old age homes foster intergenerational connections and provide emotional support to the elderly. Volunteers engage in activities like storytelling, games, and conversations, aiming to bring joy and companionship to senior citizens. These visits help bridge the gap between generations, promoting mutual understanding and respect while enhancing the emotional well-beingoftheelderly.
